Albert Einstein

  • 1879 – Albert Einstein is born in Ulm, Germany.
  • 1884 – Shows an early interest in science; receives a compass from his father.
  • 1896 – Enrolls at the Swiss Federal Polytechnic School in Zurich.
  • 1901 – Becomes a Swiss citizen and graduates from the Polytechnic.
  • 1905 – Publishes the “Annus Mirabilis” papers, including the theory of special relativity and the famous equation E=mc².
  • 1909 – Becomes a professor at the University of Zurich.
  • 1915 – Completes the general theory of relativity.
  • 1921 – Wins the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.
  • 1933 – Emigrates to the United States to escape Nazi Germany; joins the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton.
  • 1939 – Signs a letter to President Roosevelt warning about Nazi atomic research, prompting the U.S. to investigate nuclear weapons.
  • 1955 – Dies in Princeton, New Jersey.
